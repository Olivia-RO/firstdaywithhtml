What are dogs?

Dogs are an omnivorous mammal belonging to the family Canidae. Domestic dogs (Canis lupus familiaris) were domesticated around 15,000 years ago by nomadic hunter-gathers. There is no specific place thought to domesticate dogs, but there’s evidence that the most plausible places are Central Asia, East Asia, and Western Europe.

  • Fun Fact! The Bonn-Oberkassel dog was discovered in 1914 with two human skeletons dating back 14,500 years ago. Originally thought to be the mandible of a wolf, it was reexamined and reclassified as a domestic dog around 7 months of age buried with its owners.

Why did dogs get domesticated?

Wolves are a powerful tool for hunting, and hunter-gathers needed all the help they could get. Feeding the wolves and breeding the nicer, more human-friendly wolves together gave way to the modern dog. Over time, once the need for food and shelter were relatively stable for humans, they focused on breeding certain aspects of dogs to create the 360 officially recognized dog breeds.

  • Fun Fact! The Saluki is speculated to be one of the oldest dog breeds, dating back 329 BC in Ancient Egypt. Supposedly, there are also carvings found in southern Iraq that looks like the Saluki, dating back to 7000 BC.

What are dogs doing now?

Dogs have a multitude of jobs, from police work to service, and even lifeguards! Of course, dogs can also be fine just being pets, they are after all man’s best friend.

  • Fun Fact! There are three types of work dogs can do including working dogs, service dogs, and therapy dogs. Each job has certain breeds they prefer, most likely because they were most breed to fit that task.

What are the pros of owning dogs?

Each breed of dogs have certain pros and cons, but general pros include:

  • They can make you more physically active in life.
  • Studies have shown that dogs help with stress, depression, and anxiety (although they are not a cure).
  • Dogs can keep you safe by barking at would-be burglars or even attacking threats. They also have a heightened sense of hearing and smell and can detect things humans can’t.
  • They can be very affectionate and arent normally picky about the attention they gain.
  • They are very loyal and loving.
  • Having a dog with younger kids helps the kids with allergies by changing the dust makeup in your house.

What are the cons of owning dogs?

Dogs aren’t a perfect pet for everyone, and owners need to weigh the pros and cons. Some cons are:

  • Dogs are expensive. Certain breeds go up to $5000, but most even out around $1000. Things like food, vet bills, and toys gather up as well.
  • Dogs need attention and time and need access to outside areas.
  • If getting a puppy, they need to be at least potty trained or else later in life they'll wreck the house.
  • They will chew objects if they don’t have toys.
  • Some dog breeds shed a lot, and their fur will get over clothes and floors.
  • Dogs have different personalities, and putting dogs in uncomfortable situations can make the dog attack out of fright.

Should I get a dog?

This page was really only a brief explanation of dogs. Dogs, and any pets, are living creatures and shouldn’t be treated as inanimate objects. Research is a key part of owning pets; keep in mind your budget! If you do get a pet dog, give it attention, time, and love!
